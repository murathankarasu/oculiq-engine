Compliance (GDPR · EU AI Act · KVKK)

Gender/age estimation is lawful in the EU when done like Oculiq does it: on-device, no images retained, aggregate-only output, no unique identification. It is not a prohibited AI Act category (those are race, political opinion, religion, sexual orientation) and per Commission guidance is not high-risk. Your duties as operator: inform people in the monitored area (AI Act Art. 50), run a DPIA for systematic public-space monitoring, and keep retention minimal. Oculiq enforces k-anonymity: splits for groups under 5 people are suppressed automatically. Suggested venue notice: "This area uses anonymous, on-device audience measurement (attention + aggregate demographics). No images or identities are stored. Contact: [operator]".

How measurement works

People are detected with YOLO11-pose and tracked with persistent IDs. Attention is orientation-based: head pose when the face is visible (confidence 0.85), body orientation from shoulder geometry when it isn't (confidence 0.5) — including people with their back to the camera. A person "looks" at a zone when their direction falls inside an attention cone for ≥0.4s. Perspective is auto-calibrated per camera from how people shrink with distance. Rates ship with 95% confidence intervals. This is honest proxy measurement, not eye-tracking.
